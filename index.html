Miniaturized Mechanical Testing

Testing in-service components are essential to understand their failure. This helps design new materials, manufacturing methods, and parts. Miniaturised testing allows site-specific testing, which is vital to comprehend material under the thermal and mechanical stresses during manufacturing. Additive manufacturing also demands site-specific testing because of its inherent nature.

In-situ Testing

In-situ characterization augments the information obtained as it is carried out under real-time loading. The vital information collected is essential for identifying the failure mechanism and allows material manufacturers to design new materials. Here it is employed for measuring single crystal elastic constants for new materials that are challenging and expensive to determine otherwise.

Why go smaller

Advantages of Miniaturised Testing

Numbers don't lie. Here's what switching to miniature specimens actually delivers.

70%

Reduction in Material Cost

Miniature specimens require a fraction of the raw material. Critical for expensive alloys, AM metals, and rare ceramics where every gram counts.

60%

Reduction in Sample Preparation Time

Less material to machine means faster turnaround. More specimens per batch, faster design iteration, and quicker time-to-insight.

Seamless Integration with Characterisation Equipment

Compact fixture design fits directly on XRD goniometers, Raman stages, and optical microscopes — run mechanical and structural characterisation simultaneously.

90%

Saving in Printing Time & Cost for AM

Additive manufacturing is expensive. Miniature specimens reduce printing time and cost by up to 90% — making iterative process qualification actually feasible.
